DST Center for Policy Research (CPR) on Disaster Management, Energy Transition, and Tribal Education

Department of Science and Technology

About us

The DST CPR at NISER will primarily focus on different aspects of Energy Transition. The CPR will focus on how to devise appropriate policy measures to increase the production and adoption of clean energy at household and firms and office spaces. The CPR will review the existing policy measures and empirical studies to find out the effectiveness of these policy measures to increase the production of clean energy and suggest appropriate policy measures in this regard. Policy measures adopted by the major clean energy producers of the world will be reviewed to examine the feasibility of the adoption of these policies in the Indian context. The CPR will focus on eastern India covering Odisha, West Bengal, Bihar, Chhattisgarh, Jharkhand. It will interact with the energy department, science and technology departments and all stakeholders of the energy sector of all these states and national ministries to find out the challenges faced by these states. Based on the peer review of global literature on energy transition it will suggest suitable policy intervention to increase the production and adoption of clean energy by all sections of the society. It has started operating since April 2023.

Broad Objectives

The broad objectives of the DST-CPR at NISER will be as follows:
  • To assess the Nature and determinants of energy transition in India. We shall examine the effectiveness of the policy measures in promoting clean energy production. We shall assess the implications of phasing out of coal on the coal producing states in eastern India and suggest appropriate policy measures.
  • To examine the reasons behind the low transition rate of tribal children to higher and technical education in the eastern Indian states. We shall examine the association between the access to infrastructure, implementation of Forest Rights Act and the educational achievement of tribal children. We shall examine the effectiveness of technological intervention such as (ICT) for tribal education and suggest measures to improve them.
  • To examine the sectoral innovation structure in energy transition and tribal education. To evaluate the innovations taking place in the field of energy transition and tribal education and suggest necessary policy recommendations to strengthen the sectoral innovation systems. The DST CPR will critically evaluate the policy interventions, interact with all stakeholders (researchers, manufacturers, sellers, and end users) to come up with appropriate policy suggestions.
  • To leverage data science in understanding large heterogeneous data and identifying correlations between them. We shall use machine learning techniques to find the indicators as well as impacts of the policies on energy transition as well as suggest new policy measures based on the feedback received.
